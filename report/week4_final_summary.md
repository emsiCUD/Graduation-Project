## 5. Key findings

1. **Hierarchy is NOT monotonic on TEST.** Actual order: BiLSTM v2 (0.5902) < LR + char (0.6183) < PhoBERT (0.6618). BiLSTM v2 trails LR by -0.0281 despite a competitive dev score (0.6195, vs LR dev 0.6392). The dev→test gap on BiLSTM v2 (-0.0293) is wider than PhoBERT's (-0.0246) — static PhoW2V embeddings can't absorb the OFFENSIVE-class distribution shift between dev and test. PhoBERT (contextual) is the only DL model that genuinely beats LR here.
2. Even with strong regularisation (LSTM dropout 0.5, head dropout 0.6, weight_decay 1e-3), BiLSTM v2 trails LR on TEST by -0.0281. It led on DEV (0.6195) but lost 0.0293 in generalisation. Static word embeddings + a 3.5M-param BiLSTM can't beat a 25k-feature char-ngram LR once you control for distribution shift.
3. PhoBERT improves OFFENSIVE F1 by +0.0398 and HATE F1 by +0.0738 — the two minority classes where contextual sub-word embeddings + transformer attention pay off most.
4. **Persistent failure mode**: OFFENSIVE → CLEAN false negatives are common to all 3 models:
   - LR: 149 / 444 OFFENSIVE samples (33.6%)
   - BiLSTM v2: 134 / 444 (30.2%)
   - PhoBERT: 148 / 444 (33.3%)
   The fact that this rate (~30-34%) is similar across linearly-separable (LR), recurrent (BiLSTM), and transformer (PhoBERT) architectures provides strong empirical evidence that the bottleneck lies in dataset annotation rather than model capacity. This estimates an upper bound on achievable OFFENSIVE F1 around ~67-70% with current annotations, suggesting that future improvements require either re-annotation with stricter guidelines or allowing soft labels for borderline cases.
5. **Week-3 expectations were optimistic.** The Week-3 bridge projected BiLSTM ≈ 0.65-0.68 and PhoBERT ≈ 0.70-0.75 (dev). Actuals fell short: BiLSTM v2 dev 0.6195 / test 0.5902, PhoBERT dev 0.6864 / test 0.6618. This is not a modelling failure but the annotation ceiling from (4): with ~33% of OFFENSIVE crossing the CLEAN boundary even for the best model, no architecture reaches the projected range on the current labels. The projection assumed a cleaner label distribution than ViHSD actually provides.

## 6. Failure modes — hypotheses for Week 5 analysis

- **H1 (annotation ambiguity)**: OFFENSIVE samples with subtle sarcasm or in-group banter may be borderline-labeled; rough estimate of agreement: per (4), ~33% of OFFENSIVE crosses the CLEAN boundary even for PhoBERT.
- **H2 (short-comment difficulty)**: comments with length ≤ 2 (filtered out of TRAINING but kept in DEV/TEST) carry teencode/slurs that the model never trained against.
- **H3 (code-switched English-Vietnamese slurs)**: PhoBERT-v2's vocab is Vietnamese-centric; English profanity may decompose into uninformative sub-words.
- **H4 (confidence-calibration mismatch)**: high-confidence wrong predictions are usually the most informative class — `phobert_test_predictions.csv` has the probabilities.
